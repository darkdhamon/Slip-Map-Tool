Miscleanous facilities

Here the list of important (in size or reknown) infrastructures hosted by a world. It's not because an infrastrucutre is not listed for a world that it doesn't exist (there are for example a military base on every colony, but most of them are really insignificant at a galaxy scale).

  • Military Base: indicates that the world has a significant ground force stationed on it.
  • Naval Base: indicates an important spatial base or depot.
  • Prison Camp: a prison for criminals
  • Exile Camp: a dumping ground for political dissidents
  • University
  • Military Academy
  • Arcology: indicates the presence of a (or more) huge self-sufficient city (usually an underdome city)
  • Orbital tower: indicates the presence of an orbital city connected to the world by a tower.
  • Planet shield: an artificial shield suround the planet.
  • Ringworld: an artificial ring has been cronstructed around the planet.
  • Space habitats: indicates the presence of a multitude of small personnal orbital habitats.
     
Mixed population

The Natives Pop box correspond to the percentage of natives (the race that founded the colony) in the population inhabiting this world.

Economy

Major Export is the main item exported by the colony.
Major Import is the main item imported by the colony.

Here are the different type of interstellar trade goods

  • Agricultural resources (living animals, plants, food plants...)
  • Mineral resources (ferrous metal ore, nonmetal ore, raw crystals...)
  • Compounds (nitrogen compounds, raw hydrocarbons, water...)
  • Agroproducts (preserved foods, vegetables, grain, meat...)
  • Processed ores (iron, steel, alloys, isotopes...)
  • Processed compounds (raw materials typically not found in nature like petrochemicals...)
  • Weapons
  • Consumables (disposable manufactured goods like clothes, beverage, perfumes...)
  • Pharmaceuticals
  • Durable goods (tools, mechanical parts, furniture...)
  • Hi-Tech goods (starships, electronic devices, computers...)
  • Artforms (paintings, sculpture, photographs, holographics...)
  • Recordings (videos, audios, movies, music, concerts...)
  • Software
  • Scientific datas (datas created by research stations, laboratories or research faculty)
  • Exotic natural resources (exotic wood, exotic food, exotic animal companion...)
  • Prototypes Manufactured Goods (new manufactured good)
  • Uniques (specific items that cannot be duplicated like ancient artifacts, specimens of alien culture...)
